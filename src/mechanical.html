Finger Slider Module Prototype

For my design team, I performed literature and patent reviews on hand rehabilitation devices and designed a finger slider module specifically for finger flexion and extension exercises. The system is designed to be modular in order to incorporate different devices for different hand rehabilitation exercises. I modeled the prototype in SolidWorks and then 3D printed it to test for fit and function. The two piece base and slider design provides customizability for different finger sizes and allows for easy assembly and disassembly. Further iteration will involve refining the ergonomics and functionality for comfort and integrating electrical components such as time-of-flight (ToF) sensors or force sensors to measure finger position and pressure. The movement data can then be translated into a game interface to provide real-time rehab feedback and improve exercise adherence.

Features: SolidWorks, 3D Printing, Mechanical Prototyping, Human-Centered Design

Spring-Actuated Knee Rehabilitation Device

In this project I worked with a team to assess the range of motion (ROM) and angular velocity for healthy and unhealthy knees, while designing a solution to address the impairment. I used MediaPipe as a tool to track knee motion and used MATLAB to process the data and calculate the ROM and angular velocity. A final design was proposed for a spring-actuated device that provided additional force to propel the knee from flexion to extension. The concept involves an adjustable spring mechanism to tailor towards the patient's strength and rehabilitation progress.

Features: MediaPipe, MATLAB, SolidWorks, Markerless Motion Tracking, Biomechanics

Motion & Finite Element Analysis

This was my first exposure to SolidWorks beyond basic CAD modeling. I learned how to use the motion simulation to perform an analysis on a crank slider mechanism to generate displacement plots. I also learned about finite element analysis (FEA) and mesh convergences to analyze various mechanical components for stress and strain. This accumulated to a design competition where I used those techniques to optimize a yoke design to withstand another competitor's yoke pulling load.

Features: SolidWorks, Motion Simulation, Finite Element Analysis (FEA), Stress Optimization
